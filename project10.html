Our Story

The Beginning

A journey of
a thousand fires

Marcela Voss grew up in Portugal where her grandmother cooked every meal over an open hearth. That memory became the compass of her life. After culinary school in Paris, she spent a decade travelling fire traditions across six continents.

In 1998, she channelled everything she had learned into a 40-seat restaurant on West 18th Street. The name was simple. The ambition was not.

The Space

Designed for
contemplation

The dining room was designed by architect Helena Cruz to feel like a lantern — warm, contained, a world apart. Raw steel, aged oak, hand-thrown ceramics from a studio in Oaxaca.

At the centre, visible through the open pass, the hearth burns every evening from five o'clock.

Our Sourcing

Rooted in the
land around us

We work with 18 farms within 200 miles of Manhattan — every visit, every relationship built personally over years. Our menu changes weekly because we cook what is genuinely at its peak.

Our rooftop hosts 24 beehives whose honey appears on the dessert menu every night of the year.

Questions

Frequently Asked

How far in advance should I book?+
Two weeks for weekends, one week for weeknights. Same-day tables occasionally available — call after 4 PM to inquire.
Is there a dress code?+
Smart casual. We ask that guests avoid sportswear, trainers, and shorts during evening service.
What is your cancellation policy?+
Cancellations accepted up to 48 hours before arrival at no charge. Within 48 hours, a $75/person fee applies for tasting menu reservations.
Can you accommodate dietary requirements?+
Yes. Vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free and most other requirements with 24 hours' notice. Include details in your reservation notes.
Is the tasting menu available for one person?+
The tasting menu must be chosen by the whole table — it's a shared journey. For mixed preferences, we recommend à la carte.
Do you have private dining?+
Yes — The Embers Room seats up to 14 exclusively, seven nights a week. Email private@emberandash.com to begin planning.
Can I bring my own wine?+
Yes, with a corkage fee of $65 per bottle. Please notify us in advance if you plan to bring wine.
Are children welcome?+
Warmly welcome. Children's menu available on request. Evenings typically last 2–3 hours.
